Glycine

Group, Alien-Protein-code, Agent, Formula,
Neutral, C2-H5-NO2
Weight, Nature, Role, Sidechain,
75.067 g·mol−1, Hydrobophic, Glycine is integral to the formation of alpha-helices in secondary protein structure due to its compact form.it is the most abundant amino acid in collagen triple-helices. Glycine is also an inhibitory neurotransmitter – interference with its release within the spinal cord (such as during a Clostridium tetani infection) can cause spastic paralysis due to uninhibited muscle contraction Glycine functions as a bidentate ligand for many metal ions. In most organisms, the enzyme serine hydroxymethyltransferase catalyses this transformation via the cofactor pyridoxal phosphate. glycine can also be derived from threonine, choline or hydroxyproline via inter-organ metabolism of the liver and kidneys.
