119.120 g·mol−1, Polar and uncharged, The most common small motifs formed are based on interactions with serine: ST turns, ST motifs (often at the beginning of alpha helices) and ST staples (usually at the middle of alpha helices). Enzymes involved in a typical biosynthesis of threonine include:
  • aspartokinase (AK)
  • β-aspartate semialdehyde dehydrogenase
  • homoserine dehydrogenase
  • homoserine kinase
  • threonine synthase
    • In many animals it is converted to pyruvate via threonine dehydrogenase. In humans the gene for threonine dehydrogenase is an inactive pseudogene so threonine is converted to α-ketobutyrate. This is especially important in bacteria as part of the biosynthesis of cobalamin (Vitamin B12), as the product is converted to (R)-1-aminopropan-2-ol for incorporation into the vitamin's sidechain. Threonine is used to synthesize glycine during the endogenous production of L-carnitine in the brain and liver of rats.     		Hydroxyl group,
